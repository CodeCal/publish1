Physical Topologies

CSMA/CA = Carrier Sense Multiple Access / Collision Avoidance (wireless networks)

CSMA/CD = Carrier Sense Multiple Access / Collision Detection (wired networks)

Exponential Backoff--used by both ethernet and wireless networks to enable collision avoidance/detection and deconfliction

Collision Domain

--used to shrink number of devices on carrier in order to reduce collisions

Switch

Routing

Hop Limit--used to determine which route is used.

Packet Switching--chop up packets and route--called packet switching in order to promise delivery of packets which can be reassembled in order.

Protocols that enable decentralized transfers: Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP) and Board Gateway Protocol (BGP)

World's first packet-switch network ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency...NET)

DDNS = Dynamic DNS; can avoid this by forwarding traffic from your hosted address to your dynamic address or by using "no IP" services online which do the equivalent.

Bus Topology (archaic)

everything is connected by a single bus -- coaxial cable

Ring Topology (archaic)

--exactly what it sounds like. each computer is connected to two computers.

unidirectional or bidirectional

Star Topology

--central device is switch or hub

central device is single point of failure

Mesh Topology

--each device is connected to every other device by separate cabling

used in wide area networks (WANs) for redundancy. Expensive install.

Note: most networks are a hybrid type of physical topology
